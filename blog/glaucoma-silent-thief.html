Glaucoma: Stopping the 'Silent Thief of Sight'

By Dr. Jeenu Priya Tyagi • Published: Feb 2025

Glaucoma is often called the "Silent Thief of Sight." It is a devastating eye condition that damages the optic nerve, primarily caused by abnormally high pressure inside the eye (Intraocular Pressure).

The tragedy of glaucoma is that, like high blood pressure, you cannot feel it. By the time a patient notices a loss in their peripheral (side) vision, irrecoverable damage has already occurred.

Who is at Risk?

You urgently need regular pressure checks if you:

  • Are over 40 years of age.
  • Have a family history of glaucoma.
  • Have high myopia (nearsightedness).
  • Suffer from diabetes or high blood pressure.

How We Treat Glaucoma at Haripriya

While glaucoma cannot be cured, it can absolutely be stopped. Because we are a premium facility in Meerut serving the NCR region, we use gold-standard non-contact tonometry to measure your pressure effortlessly.

If diagnosed, treatment usually starts with medicated prescription eye drops that help fluid drain or reduce fluid production in the eye. If drops are insufficient, Dr. Jeenu Priya Tyagi can perform Selective Laser Trabeculoplasty (SLT) or surgical drainage procedures to rapidly bring the pressure down and save your sight.
